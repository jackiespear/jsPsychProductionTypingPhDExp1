Purpose: This experiment examines your ability to remember words. Your performance will be compared to computational models of semantic memory. This is done so in an effort to assess if they are descriptive models of human behavior.

Procedure: In this experiment, you will be presented with a list of words, one at a time. Afterward, you will be shown a set of words, some of which you will have studied in the list, and some of which will be new. Your task will be to indicate whether each word did or did not appear in the study phase. All answers will be collected by the computer.

Risks and benefits: There are typically no risks involved, greater than that which you might experience in the normal conduct of your everyday. If you become tired, you should take a break. You are free to discontinue participation at any time and with no consequences. You will benefit from participating by gaining experience with scientific research in psychology.

Your privacy: Digital data files will be anonymous and kept indefinitely on a password protected laboratory computer. Hard copies of your data files will be anonymous and kept indefinitely in a file cabinet in the Cognitive Science laboratory at the University of Manitoba. This consent form will be kept in a cabinet in the Cognitive Science laboratory at the University of Manitoba. It will be destroyed on April 1, 2023.

You will be debriefed and then you are free to leave the experiment without penalty. If you withdraw part way through the experiment, your data will be automatically deleted. If you withdraw after completing the experiment, your data will not be deleted as there is no way to link your identity to your response data.
